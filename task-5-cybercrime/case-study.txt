Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a widely used cybercrime technique where attackers attempt to deceive individuals into sharing confidential information such as passwords, OTPs, or banking details.

In most phishing incidents, users receive emails or messages that seem to come from trusted organizations like banks or popular companies. These messages usually include a link that directs users to a fraudulent website designed to look authentic. When users enter their personal information on these fake sites, the attackers capture and misuse the data.

Phishing attacks can result in serious consequences, including financial losses, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal data. Many people become victims because the messages appear legitimate and often create a sense of urgency, prompting users to act quickly without verifying the source.
